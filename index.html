MP Safa Al Hashim: “we decided the body responsible for that is the supreme council for a family”

February 18, 2020

Safaa Al-Hashim, chair of the Parliamentary Committee for Family and Women, said the committee is working on finalizing the report on domestic abuse laws before Parliament starts.

“As regards its implementation, we decided the body responsible for that is the supreme council for a family due to its specialization, and being the administrative pillar of the protection centers in charge of receiving, following up, listening to and sheltering victims”, she noted.

MP Al-Hashim said in a comment that the committee will continue studying law articles, and one of the crucial high points of the law is the law asserting the constitutional principles referred to in Article 9 of the Constitution which makes the family the cornerstone of society.

She asserted the law retains the family’s integrity and solidifies its ties and protects motherhood and infancy. The security stipulated in family law is the key issue that is opposed to domestic violence.

Furthermore, MP Al-Hashim concluded that the law covers an article setting up a coordinating committee with respective government bodies and civil society and that the role of the committee was already recognized and established.
